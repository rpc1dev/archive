How to flash your Toshiba SD-C2202 firmware to RPC-1 firmware

Carefull these firmware update are ONLY for the Toshiba SD-C2202. Using these firmware with any other model would make your drive unusable. The Toshiba SD-C2202 is a model 4X/24X, present in many laptop, in particular those from Sony, Toshiba, HP, IBM, Dell, Asus...

How to know your firmware version and know if your drive is locked

Use the Driveinfo 1.02 utility.
In fact, there is some 2202 who are shipped originaly with unlocked RPC-1 firmware (it's very rare). That will be stupid to flash a drive who is already unlocked.


Bad luck! You have to flash your drive.
You are lucky don't flash your drive. You just need to unlock your DVD software

 

Know if your drive is in master or slave position.

The Toshiba SD-C2202 has a big problem (other laptop DVD players too), it don't have any jumper to set the drive in master or slave position. In fact, this position is stored in the firmware himself. The firmware 1R17 is a master firmware, and 1T17 a slave one.
To know very easily your drive position use the utility Mhsystat 1.03.
Start the utility and select the SCSI/ATAPI button.
If your drive is in position 0, it's a master drive go to the next part to load the 1R17 firmware.
If your drive is on 1 it's a slave drive, you will have to use the 1T17 firmware.


Here the drive is in master position

Flash the firmware

After the Master/slave verification you have now to load your correct firmware.

Special 1R17 firmware for drives originaly in master position.
Special 1T17 firmware for drives originaly in slave position

You have just to extract the files on a floppy and boot on it (sometime, you will have to make some changes in your laptop BIOS to boot on a floppy).
Before flashing be sure there is no disc in the DVD drive.
Connect your laptop to the AC power (more secure).

Now flash your drive!

Warning!!! Flashing a firmware is a very sensitive operation. Don't move the mouse and don't touch your keyboard during the flash, and DO NOT STOP in any case the flash utility when started, you could damage your drive definitively. REMEMBER: This is at your own risk!!!!

Verify the drive flashed correctly


Once back to Windows, check your drive with driveinfo.
Go also to the configuration panel, click on system, select your DVD. Click on the DMA option if unchecked. That will improve greatly your drive performance.


Region free!!
Check the DMA option

Your drive is now region free!!

Unlock your DVD software

Of course some dumbs will say me "hey, i have flashed my drive but i still can't play other region DVD". Of course you have also to unlock your DVD software.
The DVD players generally shipped with laptop are hard to unlock. There is 2 region changer available :

  • IBM thinkpad Region Changer - For Thinkpad 770DVD,but in fact should works with other IBM laptop
  • DVD Region Wizard – Toshiba laptop

These utilities are available at http://www.dvdutils.com

For other my recommendation for you is to use the excellent PowerDVD 2.55 and use DVD genie available at www.inmatrix.com
